chocolate lava cake

rich, indulgent taste, mood-boosting qualities, versatility in desserts, and satisfying, comforting sweetness.

Ingredients List

  • 1. 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 4 oz (113g) semi-sweet or dark chocolate (chopped)
  • 1 cup (120g) powdered sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup (32g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

    • Instruction

      1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Grease 4–6 small ramekins (depending on size) with butter or cooking spray. Lightly dust them with cocoa powder to prevent sticking.
      2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the butter and chopped chocolate. Microwave in 20-second intervals, stirring after each, until smooth. Alternatively, you can melt the mixture in a heatproof bowl over simmering water (double boiler method).
      3. Add the powdered sugar to the melted chocolate mixture and whisk until well combined.
      4. Whisk in the eggs and egg yolks until the mixture is smooth and shiny.
      5. Gently stir in the flour, vanilla extract (optional), and a pinch of salt. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix.
      6. Evenly divide the batter between the prepared ramekins, filling each about 3/4 full.
      7. Place the ramekins on a baking sheet and bake for 12–14 minutes, or until the edges are firm but the centers are still soft and slightly jiggly. The tops should be set.
      8. Let the cakes cool in the ramekins for 1–2 minutes. Carefully run a knife around the edges to loosen the cakes, then invert them onto serving plates.
      9. Serve immediately with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, fresh berries, or a dollop of whipped cream.

    Preparation & Cooking Time

      preparation time: 15 minutes
      cooking time: 12-14 minutes

    Serving Size

    The serving size for chocolate lava cake is typically 4 to 6 cakes, depending on the size of the ramekins used. Each cake serves one person.

    Tips & Variations

      Don’t Overbake: Keep an eye on the cakes. The edges should be firm, but the center should still be slightly jiggly for that molten effect.
      Make Ahead: You can prepare the batter and refrigerate it in the ramekins for up to a day in advance. Just bake when ready, adding 1–2 extra minutes.
      Grease Well: Grease and dust the ramekins with cocoa powder to ensure the cakes release smoothly without sticking.
      Chocolate Flavor Varieties: Use milk chocolate for a sweeter taste, or go for dark chocolate (70% cocoa or higher) for a richer, more intense flavor.
      Filled Lava Cakes: Add a surprise center by inserting a square of caramel, peanut butter, or white chocolate into the middle of the batter before baking.
      Flavored Lava Cakes: Add a teaspoon of instant espresso powder for a mocha twist or a dash of cinnamon for a spiced flavor. Infuse the batter with liqueurs like Baileys, Grand Marnier, or Kahlua for an adult version.
